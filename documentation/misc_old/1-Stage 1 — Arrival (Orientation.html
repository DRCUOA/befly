How to read here

This site is designed around a few simple principles that honor your time and attention

1

One essay at a time

When you open an essay, that's all you see. No sidebar of recommendations. No "related posts." No distractions competing for your attention. Just the piece you chose to read.

The navigation reappears only when you finish or choose to leave.

2

Read at your own pace

No reading time estimates. No progress bars. No artificial urgency. The text is patient. Take as long as you need.

Some pieces are meant to be read in twenty minutes. Others might take an hour, or require multiple sessions. Trust your instinct.

3

Nothing will interrupt you

No pop-ups asking for your email. No cookie banners. No chat widgets. No analytics tracking your scroll depth. You're here to read, and I respect that.

If you want to stay connected, there's a simple subscribe option at the end of each essay. One click, no pressure.

4

Trust the silence

White space isn't wasted space. The pauses between paragraphs give you room to think. The margins give your eyes a place to rest.

This emptiness is intentional. It's part of the reading experience.

5

Leave when you're ready

If an essay isn't resonating, you can leave at any time. No guilt, no friction. Your attention is yours to give.

The work that matters will find you when the timing is right.

About the writer

I write about attention, craft, and the small rebellions that make life worth living. My background is in design and philosophy, which means I think a lot about how things work and why they matter.

This site exists because I wanted a place to publish work that couldn't fit anywhere else—essays too long for social media, too slow for most publications, too personal for professional platforms.

I believe in taking time. I believe in revising until the words are right. I believe that readers deserve work that respects their intelligence and their attention.

If that resonates with you, welcome. I'm glad you're here.

What I believe about writing

Quality over quantity

I publish when I have something worth saying, not on a schedule. Some months bring three essays. Others bring none. The work dictates the timeline.

Depth over virality

I'm not trying to go viral or rack up shares. I'm trying to write something that matters to the handful of people who need to read it.

Clarity over cleverness

Good writing serves the reader, not the writer's ego. If a sentence sounds impressive but obscures meaning, it gets cut.

Honesty over performance

I write what I actually think, not what I think will get applause. Sometimes that means admitting uncertainty. That's okay.

How this site works

Transparency about the technology and choices behind the experience

No tracking, no analytics

I don't track page views, time on site, scroll depth, or any other metrics. I don't use Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, or any third-party tracking scripts.

I have no idea how many people read these essays, and I prefer it that way. The work either resonates or it doesn't. Numbers won't change that.

Static site, fast loading

This is a static website—just HTML, CSS, and minimal JavaScript. No database queries, no server-side rendering, no complex frameworks.

Pages load in under a second. The entire site is under 200KB. Fast by design, not by accident.

Accessible to everyone

Semantic HTML, proper heading hierarchy, high contrast ratios, keyboard navigation, screen reader support.

If you encounter any accessibility issues, please let me know. Good design should be inclusive design.

Privacy by default

If you subscribe to the newsletter, your email is stored securely and never shared. You can unsubscribe anytime with one click.

No cookies, no local storage, no fingerprinting. Your visit here leaves no trace.

Technology should serve the reader, not extract value from them. Every technical choice here reflects that principle.
