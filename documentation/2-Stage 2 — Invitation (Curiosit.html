What the metadata means

Every essay includes a few pieces of information to help you decide what to read

Collection

Each essay belongs to one of three thematic collections: Attention, Craft, or Rebellion. This helps you understand the essay's focus without reading it first.

Date

When the essay was published. Not when it was written—some pieces sit for months before they're ready. The date tells you recency, not relevance.

Word count

Exact word count, not rounded. Some readers prefer short pieces, others want depth. This lets you choose based on your available time and attention.

Read time

Estimated at 280 words per minute—a comfortable reading pace that allows for comprehension and reflection.

Note: This is a guideline, not a timer. Read at whatever pace feels right.
